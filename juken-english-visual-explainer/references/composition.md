# Composition Module

Use this module for free writing, Japanese-to-English translation, summaries, opinion essays, and user-written English.

## Core Goal

Help the learner understand how to improve the answer and reuse the reasoning in future writing. Do not merely replace the answer with polished English.

## Analyze Before Revising

- Identify the task type, required content, expected length, and register.
- Read `scoring-rubric.md` before assigning a score or score estimate.
- Preserve valid ideas from the learner's answer.
- Separate correctness from stylistic preference.
- Distinguish errors from acceptable but improvable wording.
- Provide multiple valid expressions when useful.

## Recommended Feedback Dimensions

- Task fulfillment: whether the answer addresses every required point and follows word-count or format conditions.
- Content: whether the claim, explanation, examples, and required information are sufficient and relevant.
- Logic and organization: whether claim, reason, example, contrast, and conclusion connect coherently.
- Grammar and syntax: tense, agreement, articles, countability, word order, clauses, and modifiers.
- Vocabulary and expression: meaning, collocation, register, precision, and naturalness.
- Mechanics: spelling, punctuation, and readability.
- Exam strategy: robust wording that the learner can reproduce under time pressure.

## Scoring Rules

- Use an official university rubric when the user supplies one or a reliable official rubric is available for the specified university, year, and task.
- Otherwise label the evaluation `採点目安` or `推定評価`. Do not claim an official score.
- Do not apply one university's weights to all universities.
- Show category-level feedback before an overall estimate.
- Distinguish a disqualifying task mismatch from smaller language errors.
- For free composition, score communication and logic separately from grammar.
- For Japanese-to-English translation, score meaning coverage separately from grammar and naturalness.
- For summary writing, score retained information, omitted information, paraphrase quality, logic, and word-count compliance.

## Useful UI Patterns

- Side-by-side original and improved answer.
- Revision highlights with filters: `内容`, `文法`, `語法`, `自然さ`, `構成`.
- Rubric panel with the scoring source: `大学公式基準`, `提示された基準`, or `共通ルーブリックによる採点目安`.
- Category diagnostics for `課題適合`, `内容`, `論理構成`, `語彙・表現`, `文法・構文`, `表記・条件`.
- Sentence-role map: `主張`, `理由`, `具体例`, `結論`.
- Alternative-expression selector grouped by difficulty or nuance.
- Scoring panel only when a rubric is known or clearly labeled as an estimate.
- Reusable-expression panel with short example sentences.

## Required Content for User Answers

- Preserve the original answer.
- Show the improved answer.
- Explain meaningful revisions.
- Identify strengths as well as weaknesses.
- Provide a concise model answer.
- Include reusable phrases when relevant.
- Explain whether the score is official or estimated.

## Avoid

- Overcorrecting every sentence into advanced prose.
- Treating all stylistic alternatives as errors.
- Giving only a score.
- Hiding the reason for revisions.
