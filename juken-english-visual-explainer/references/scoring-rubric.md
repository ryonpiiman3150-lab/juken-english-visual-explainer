# Entrance-Exam Composition Scoring Rubric

Use this reference for free composition, Japanese-to-English translation, summary writing, picture description, and user-answer feedback.

## Important Limit

There is no single public scoring table shared by all Japanese universities. Weighting varies by university, year, and task. Use a university's published rubric when available. Otherwise present a diagnostic estimate, not an official score.

## Default Diagnostic Rubric

Use these axes when no task-specific official rubric is available.

| Axis | Check |
| --- | --- |
| `課題適合` | Answer the actual prompt. Follow required stance, information, word count, format, and specified expressions. |
| `内容` | Include relevant ideas, reasons, examples, and required information. Avoid copying or unrelated writing. |
| `論理構成` | Keep the position clear and consistent. Connect claims, reasons, examples, contrast, and conclusion coherently. |
| `語彙・表現` | Use context-appropriate wording, collocations, register, and concise expression. |
| `文法・構文` | Control tense, agreement, articles, countability, word order, clauses, and sentence structure. |
| `表記・条件` | Check spelling, punctuation, readability, word count, and other formal conditions. |

Display category diagnostics before any overall estimate. Use labels such as `良好`, `要改善`, and `大きな減点リスク`, or an A-E estimate. Explain that the result is an estimate unless it directly follows an official rubric.

## Task-Specific Adjustments

### Free Composition

- Prioritize prompt compliance, position, reasons, concrete support, coherence, vocabulary, grammar, and mechanics.
- Treat an unrelated response or mere copying of the prompt as a major problem.
- Do not let small grammar errors erase a strong argument unless the meaning becomes unclear.

### Japanese-to-English Translation

- Check meaning coverage and nuance first.
- Then check sentence skeleton, grammar, vocabulary, collocation, and naturalness.
- Encourage rewriting the Japanese into an easier-to-express meaning before translating when useful.
- Accept multiple valid translations.

### Summary Writing

- Check whether the central point and required information remain.
- Show what to keep, omit, and paraphrase.
- Check logic and word-count compliance.

### Picture or Graph Description

- Check factual accuracy, relevant coverage, concision, vocabulary, and grammar.
- Penalize invented information or irrelevant writing.

## Published Evidence Used to Design This Rubric

The following sources justify the default axes. Do not treat any one source's weights as universal.

- Otaru University of Commerce, 2025 English evaluation points: evaluates coherent logical writing, appropriate vocabulary and expression, grammatical accuracy, concise description, spelling, punctuation, prompt relevance, and word-count strategy.  
  https://nyushi.otaru-uc.ac.jp/wp-content/uploads/sites/2/2025/03/2025point_e.pdf
- Kobe University, 2025 later schedule English evaluation points: evaluates logical English expression using appropriate vocabulary, expressions, and syntax for translation and opinion writing.  
  https://www.office.kobe-u.ac.jp/stdnt-examinavi/wp-content/uploads/2025/05/10_k_english_ito_R7.pdf
- Tottori University, 2023 English answer examples and intent: translation checks logical development, appropriate English, grammar, vocabulary, and composition; free composition checks concrete examples, reasons, content, logic, and English accuracy.  
  https://www.admissions.adm.tottori-u.ac.jp/wp-content/uploads/2023/04/R5_eigo_kaitourei.pdf
- Z-kai teacher journal, 2024: uses `課題達成`, `論理構成`, `文法`, and `語彙`, and stresses clarity of the claim and whether reasons support the opinion.  
  https://www.zkai.co.jp/solutions/teacher/ejournal/2406_writing_english/
- Z-kai University of Tokyo advice: stresses prompt compliance, grammar and usage accuracy, and logic understandable to a third-party reader.  
  https://www.zkai.co.jp/todai-exam/advice/10up-eigo-sakubun/
- Toshin writing feedback description: highlights word count, specified expressions, grammar advice, answer revision, commentary, and alternative answers.  
  https://www.toshin.com/recent/detail/RecentToshin.php?id=36

## Example of University-Specific Weighting

An Osaka Sangyo University public document for a 2024 task disclosed a rubric with `content 30`, `organization 20`, `vocabulary 20`, `grammar 25`, and `spelling/punctuation 5`. Use this only as an example that universities may weight axes differently. Do not use it as the default rubric for other universities.
